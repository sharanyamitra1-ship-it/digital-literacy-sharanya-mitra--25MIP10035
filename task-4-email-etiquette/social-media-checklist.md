5 Do's for responsible social media use as a college student.
1.Audit Your Privacy Settings: Users should check their social media privacy settings on Instagram and Facebook to stop 
unauthorized people from accessing their posts and personal details.

2.Curate a Professional Image: You should present your academic accomplishments together with your completed projects and volunteer
activities to establish a positive online presence which recruiters will find attractive.

3.Think Before You Post: Take a moment to consider if your content could be misinterpreted or if it reflects your 
values before hitting the "share" button

4.Engage Respectfully: Social media platforms provide users with tools to back their friends while they can also join 
constructive academic and professional discussions instead of getting involved in destructive online fights.

5.Verify Information: Users should verify news sources before posting any emergency updates to prevent 
unverified information from spreading through their network..


5 Don'ts for responsible social media use as a college student.
1.Do Not Share Secret Information: You should prevent the distribution of student ID images and exam paper copies and secret campus 
area pictures because they endanger your safety and academic trustworthiness.

2.Do Not Spread Negative Comments About Schools or Their Staff: You should avoid posting about your college or faculty
because these messages will remain accessible to future employers. 

3.Keep Your Location Details Private: People should stay away from showing their exact current location through geotags 
because this practice enables unknown people to follow their everyday movements.

4.Don't Engage with Trolls: Users should block or report users who break community rules instead
of responding to their hateful or provocative messages. 

5.People should wait until their emotions settle  they should wait before sharing their thoughts through social media posts because
emotional posts tend to create enduring negative effects on personal digital reputation.
