Stay Safe Online checklist for students

1.Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Wherever a university portal, Gmail account or social media handles is concerned, one must always use
  an authenticator app or SMS codes(if necessary) as a second layer of security besides a password.
  
2.Be cautious of suspicious links: While the college asks for information concerning "scholarships" or "hostel allotments", be careful while 
 clicking on any link, especially one which doesn't end in ".edu.in" or ".gov.in", as these would often lead to 
 a phishing website, aiming to gain access to your student credentials.

3.Avoid using public Wi-Fi for official tasks: Always refrain from making online financialtransactions or logging into yourstudent account using
  free public Wi-Fi available in cafes or at railway stations; consider using a personal hot spot or an encrypted VPN instead.

4.Review app permissions: In order to not let apps especially those that provide "free" study resources have unwanted access to your location, 
contacts or even microphone, regularly check your phone's settings.

5.Ignore work from home opportunities: If you receive random WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering "work from home" part-time jobs after
charging a small registration fee, beware, it is a recruitment scam.

6.Think twice before over-sharing your personal details on social media: I can’t stress enough, don’t upload photos of your hall tickets,
Aadhaar card, internship offer letters, etc. It doesn’t mattereven if you blur them – there are powerful AI programs that can actually 
detect blurred images, hence stealing your identity or taking your exams and/or assignments under false pretense.

7.Device Hygiene is a must: Set up a strong biometric lock, or a 6-digit PIN on all your gadgets, along with updating your device's OS 
and anti-virus to patch security loopholes.

8.Look for the "HTTPS" prefix and the Padlock Symbol: Always remember to first check for https:// prefix and the closed padlock icon in 
the browser address bar before you login with your student ID/password, or key in your credit/debit card details. This is to 
secure your data against prying eyes on your local network.

Financial & UPI Safety
Do not share your UPI PIN to receive money: The only two instances when a UPI PIN is required are while transferring 
funds to another account or checking your balance. If anyone asks for your PIN to "receive" cash into
your account such as prizes or refunds, you are being scammed.

Check the merchant name: Before proceeding to click the "Pay" button in your UPI application, always ensure the verified name appearing 
on your screen matches with the person or entity to whom you are paying money.

How to Report Cybercrime in India:
In case you or anyone you know gets a victim of any online scam, or a case of cyber-harassment repror to  national cyber crime portal 
(cybercrime.gov.in)Helpline: Dial 1930 This is the national grievance redressal helpline for reporting financial cyber-frauds. 
This call may lead to a quicker blocking of the funds transferred out.
